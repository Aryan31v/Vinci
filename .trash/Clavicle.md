## 1. Introduction & Definition
- The **clavicle** is a **long bone** placed **horizontally** at the **root of the neck**.
- It connects the **upper limb to the axial skeleton**.
- It functions as a **strut**, holding the shoulder laterally and permitting free movement of the upper limb.
> **Grayâ€™s concept**: The clavicle is a mechanical link that transmits forces from the upper limb to the trunk.

## 2. Side Determination
To determine the side of a clavicle:
- **Medial end**: thick, bulky, quadrangular
- **Lateral end**: flat
- **Superior surface**: smooth
- **Inferior surface**: rough
- **Medial 2/3** of shaft: convex forwards
- **Lateral 1/3** of shaft: concave forwards

## 4. Medial (Sternal) End
- Large, quadrangular
- Articulates with: Manubrium sterni, First costal cartilage
- **Joint:** Sternoclavicular (Saddle)
- **Ligaments:**
  - Anterior/Posterior sternoclavicular
  - Interclavicular
  - **Costoclavicular** (chief stabilizer)

## 5. Lateral (Acromial) End
- Flat from above downwards
- Articulates with **acromion process of scapula**
- **Joint:** Acromioclavicular (Plane)
- **Ligaments:**
  - Acromioclavicular
  - **Coracoclavicular** (Conoid + Trapezoid) - Main Stabilizer.

## 10. Applied Anatomy
