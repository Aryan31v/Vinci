## 1. Introduction & Definition
- The **clavicle** is a **long bone** placed **horizontally** at the **root of the neck**.
- It connects the **upper limb to the axial skeleton**.
- It functions as a **strut**, holding the shoulder laterally and permitting free movement of the upper limb.

> **Grayâ€™s concept**: The clavicle is a mechanical link that transmits forces from the upper limb to the trunk.

---

## 2. Side Determination (Vishram Singh)
To determine the side of a clavicle:
- Two ends: **medial (sternal)** and **lateral (acromial)**
- **Medial end**: thick, bulky, quadrangular
- **Lateral end**: flat
- **Superior surface**: smooth
- **Inferior surface**: rough
- **Medial 2/3** of shaft: convex forwards
- **Lateral 1/3** of shaft: concave forwards

## 4. Medial (Sternal) End
### Description
- Large, quadrangular
- Articulates with:
  - Manubrium sterni
  - First costal cartilage

### Joint
- **Sternoclavicular joint**
- Type: **Saddle synovial joint**

### Ligament Attachments (Grayâ€™s)
- Anterior sternoclavicular ligament
- Posterior sternoclavicular ligament
- Interclavicular ligament
- **Costoclavicular ligament** (chief stabilizer)

> This is the **only bony articulation between upper limb and trunk**.

---

## 5. Lateral (Acromial) End
### Description
- Flat from above downwards
- Articulates with **acromion process of scapula**

### Joint
- **Acromioclavicular joint**
- Type: **Plane synovial joint**

### Ligament Attachments
- Acromioclavicular ligament
- **Coracoclavicular ligament**, consisting of:
  - **Conoid ligament** (medial, vertical)
  - **Trapezoid ligament** (lateral, oblique)

> **Vishram Singh**: Coracoclavicular ligament is the **main stabilizer** of the joint.
