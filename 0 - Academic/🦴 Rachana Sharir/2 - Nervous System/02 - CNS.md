## üß† 2. The Brain (Encephalon)
*Weight: ~1.4 kg (2% of body weight, consumes 20% of oxygen).*

### A. Forebrain (Prosencephalon)

#### 1. Telencephalon (Cerebrum)
- **Structure:** Two hemispheres separated by the *Longitudinal Fissure*, connected by the *Corpus Callosum*.
- **Lobes & Functions:**
    - **Frontal:** Motor control, Broca's area (Speech production), Personality, Judgment (PFC).
    - **Parietal:** Somatosensory cortex (Touch, Pain, Temp), Spatial awareness.
    - **Temporal:** Auditory cortex, Wernicke's area (Speech comprehension), Memory (Hippocampus).
    - **Occipital:** Visual processing.
- **Basal Ganglia:** (Caudate, Putamen, Globus Pallidus) - Control of voluntary motor movements, procedural learning, habits.

#### 2. Diencephalon (The "Between Brain")
- **Thalamus:** The "Relay Station." All senses (except smell) pass through here to the cortex.
- **Hypothalamus:** The "Master Controller" of Homeostasis (Autonomic Nervous System, Endocrine System, Body Temp, Hunger/Thirst).
- **Epithalamus:** Contains Pineal Gland (Melatonin/Circadian rhythm).

### B. Midbrain (Mesencephalon)
- **Tectum:** Superior Colliculi (Visual reflexes) & Inferior Colliculi (Auditory reflexes).
- **Tegmentum:** Red Nucleus & Substantia Nigra (Dopamine production - Parkinson's relevance).
- **Cerebral Peduncles:** Motor tracts descending from cortex.

### C. Hindbrain (Rhombencephalon)

#### 1. Pons ("The Bridge")
- Connects Cerebellum to Brainstem.
- Contains nuclei for **CN V, VI, VII, VIII**.
- Respiratory centers: Pneumotaxic & Apneustic centers.

#### 2. Medulla Oblongata
- Continues as Spinal Cord.
- **Vital Centers:** Cardiac, Vasomotor, Respiratory.
- **Pyramids:** Site of *Decussation* (Crossing over) of motor tracts (Why Left brain controls Right body).
- Contains nuclei for **CN IX, X, XI, XII**.

#### 3. Cerebellum ("The Little Brain")
- **Function:** Balance, Coordination, Fine motor skills, Muscle tone.
- **Structure:** Vermis (center) + 2 Hemispheres. Arbor Vitae (Tree of life - white matter pattern).

## üêç 3. The Spinal Cord (Medulla Spinalis)
*The Information Highway.*

### A. Gross Anatomy
- **Extent:** Foramen Magnum to L1/L2 Border.
- **Enlargements:** Cervical (C4-T1) for arms, Lumbar (L2-S3) for legs.
- **Terminates as:** Conus Medullaris.
- **Anchor:** Filum Terminale (Pia mater extension).
- **Nerves below L2:** Cauda Equina (Horse's tail).

### B. Cross-Sectional Anatomy
- **Grey Matter (Inner "H"):**
    - *Dorsal Horn:* Sensory input.
    - *Ventral Horn:* Motor output (Alpha motor neurons).
    - *Lateral Horn (T1-L2):* Sympathetic ANS neurons.
- **White Matter (Outer Columns):**
    - Contains Ascending (Sensory) and Descending (Motor) tracts.

### C. Major Tracts (The Wiring)

| Tract | Type | Function |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Corticospinal** | Descending (Motor) | Voluntary movement (Pyramidal). |
| **Spinothalamic** | Ascending (Sensory) | Pain, Temperature, Crude touch. |
| **Dorsal Column** | Ascending (Sensory) | Fine touch, Vibration, Proprioception. |

## ü©∏ 4. Blood Supply (Crucial for Stroke)
### The Circle of Willis
- **Anterior Supply:** Internal Carotid Arteries -> Anterior & Middle Cerebral Arteries.
- **Posterior Supply:** Vertebral Arteries -> Basilar Artery -> Posterior Cerebral Arteries.
- **Communicating Arteries:** Anterior & Posterior Communicating arteries complete the circle to ensure redundancy.

## ‚öïÔ∏è 5. Applied Anatomy (Clinical)
- **Stroke (CVA):** Blockage/Bleed in Circle of Willis (esp. Middle Cerebral Artery).
- **Lumbar Puncture:** Performed at L3-L4 or L4-L5 interspace to avoid damaging the spinal cord (which ends at L1).
- **Hydrocephalus:** Blockage of CSF flow leads to brain swelling.
- **Parkinson's:** Degeneration of Substantia Nigra (Midbrain).
