## 1. Definition (Nirukti)
> *Tap Santape*
> **Meaning:** Pitta is derived from the root 'Tap' which means to heat, burn, or warm. It represents the energy of transformation, digestion, and metabolism.

## 2. Properties (Guna)
> *Pittam sasneha tikshna ushnam laghu visram saram dravam || AH.Su 1/11*
> - **Sasneha** (Slightly Oily/Unctuous)
> - **Tikshna** (Sharp/Penetrating)
> - **Ushna** (Hot)
> - **Laghu** (Light)
> - **Visram** (Fleshy smell)
> - **Saram** (Fluid/Flowing)
> - **Dravam** (Liquid)

## 3. Common Locations (Sthana)
> *Nabhih amashayah svedo lasika rudhiram rasah |
> Drik sparshanam cha pittasya nabhih atra visheshatah || AH.Su 12/2*
> - **Nabhi** (Navel/Umbilicus) - *Primary Seat*
> - **Amashaya** (Lower Stomach/Duodenum)
> - **Sveda** (Sweat)
> - **Lasika** (Lymph)
> - **Rudhira/Rakta** (Blood)
> - **Rasa** (Plasma)
> - **Drik** (Eyes)
> - **Sparshanam** (Skin)

## 4. Common Functions (Karma)
> *Pakti ushma darshanam kshut trit ruchi prabha medha dhi shaurya tanu mardavaih || AH.Su 11/2*
> - **Pakti:** Digestion.
> - **Ushma:** Body Heat/Temperature maintenance.
> - **Darshana:** Vision.
> - **Kshut:** Hunger.
> - **Trit:** Thirst.
> - **Ruchi:** Appetite/Taste.
> - **Prabha:** Lustre/Glow.
> - **Medha:** Intellect (Retention).
> - **Dhi:** Intelligence (Discrimination).
> - **Shaurya:** Courage/Valor.
> - **Tanu Mardava:** Softness of the body.

## 5. Types of Pitta (Panchavidha Pitta)

### A. Pachaka Pitta (The Digester)
> *Pachakam amashaya sthitam... anna paka krit...*
> **Location (Sthana):**
> - Between Amashaya (Stomach) and Pakvashaya (Intestine) - *Grahani*.
**Function (Karma):**
- **Digestion:** Digests food, separates essence (*Sara*) from waste (*Kitta*).
- **Foundation:** Supports all other types of Pitta ("Pitta Dhara Kala").
- **Modern Correlate:** Gastric enzymes, Bile, Pancreatic juices.
- **Diseases:** *Ajeerna* (Indigestion), *Amlapitta* (Acidity).

### B. Ranjaka Pitta (The Colorer)
> *Amashaya ashrayam pittam ranjakam rasa ranjanat...*
> **Location (Sthana):**
> - Amashaya (Stomach) & Yakrit/Pliha (Liver/Spleen).
**Function (Karma):**
- **Hemopoiesis:** Converts Rasa Dhatu (Plasma) into Rakta Dhatu (Blood) by imparting color.
- **Modern Correlate:** Intrinsic Factor, Hematinic principles in Liver.
- **Diseases:** *Pandu* (Anemia), *Kamala* (Jaundice).

### C. Sadhaka Pitta (The Thinker)
> *Buddhi medha abhimana adyaih abhipreta artha sadhanat...*
> **Location (Sthana):**
> - Hridaya (Heart/Brain connection).
**Function (Karma):**
- **Mental Faculties:** Responsible for *Buddhi* (Intellect), *Medha* (Memory), and *Abhimana* (Ego/Self-worth).
- **Determination:** Helps achieve goals (*Sadhana*).
- **Modern Correlate:** Neurotransmitters (Dopamine, Serotonin) governing drive and cognition.
- **Diseases:** Loss of memory, lack of focus, emotional instability.

### D. Alochaka Pitta (The Seer)
> *Rupa alochana samarthyam...*
> **Location (Sthana):**
> - Eyes (Drishti).
**Function (Karma):**
- **Vision:** Perception of form and color.
- **Sub-types (Dalhana):** *Chakshu Vaisheshika* (Visual perception) and *Buddhi Vaisheshika* (Visual processing/interpretation).
- **Modern Correlate:** Rhodopsin/Iodopsin pigments in Retina.
- **Diseases:** Poor vision, color blindness.

### E. Bhrajaka Pitta (The Illuminator)
> *Tvacha stham bhrajakam bhrajana tvachah...*
> **Location (Sthana):**
> - Skin (Twak).
**Function (Karma):**
- **Complexion:** Maintains skin color and luster.
- **Absorption:** Helps absorb substances applied to the skin (*Abhyanga*, *Lepa*).
- **Thermoregulation:** Radiates heat.
- **Modern Correlate:** Melanin, cutaneous blood flow.
- **Diseases:** Skin diseases (*Kushtha*), discoloration, eczema.

## ðŸ§ª 6. Supplementary Insights

### A. Mahabhuta Predominance
- **Primary:** Tejas (Fire).
- **Secondary:** Jala (Water) - responsible for its liquid (*Dravam*) and oily (*Sasneha*) nature.

### B. Pathology (Vikriti)
- **Pitta Vriddhi (Hyper):**
    - Yellow discoloration (skin/eyes/urine).
    - Excessive hunger and thirst.
    - Burning sensation (*Daha*).
    - Less sleep (*Alpanidra*).
- **Pitta Kshaya (Hypo):**
    - Loss of luster/complexion.
    - Coldness (*Stambha*).
    - Loss of digestive fire (*Mandagni*).

### C. Management
- **Tastes (Rasa):** Pacified by **Madhura** (Sweet), **Tikta** (Bitter), **Kashaya** (Astringent).
- **Primary Therapy:** **Virechana** (Purgation) expels excess Pitta from its root (Nabhi/Small Intestine).
- **Diet:** Cooling foods, Ghee (best for Pitta), Milk.
- **Lifestyle:** Avoid sun exposure, anger, and alcohol.
