## üìñ 1. Definition (‡§®‡§ø‡§∞‡•Å‡§ï‡•ç‡§§‡§ø & ‡§™‡§∞‡§ø‡§≠‡§æ‡§∑‡§æ)
The word **Tridosha** is composed of two words: **Tri** (Three) + **Dosha** (Bio-energies/Faults).
They are called "Doshas" because they have the tendency to corrupt or vitiate other bodily tissues (Dhatus) and waste products (Malas) when out of balance.

> **Sanskrit Etymology:**
> "‡§¶‡•Ç‡§∑‡§Ø‡§®‡•ç‡§§‡§ø ‡§á‡§§‡§ø ‡§¶‡•ã‡§∑‡§æ‡§É‡•§" (Dooshayanti iti doshah)
> *Meaning: That which vitiates or corrupts is called Dosha.*

However, in their normal state (Prakrita Avastha), they sustain the body:
> "‡§¶‡•ã‡§∑ ‡§ß‡§æ‡§§‡•Å ‡§Æ‡§≤ ‡§Æ‡•Ç‡§≤‡§Ç ‡§π‡§ø ‡§∂‡§∞‡•Ä‡§∞‡§Æ‡•ç‡•§" (Dosha Dhatu Mala Moolam Hi Shariram)
> *Meaning: The body is rooted in Doshas, Dhatus, and Malas.*

## ‚öôÔ∏è 4. Properties (Guna) - The Gunas
Understanding properties is the key to treatment (Treatment = Opposite Guna).

### üå™Ô∏è Vata Gunas (Properties of Vata)
> "‡§§‡§§‡•ç‡§∞ ‡§∞‡•Ç‡§ï‡•ç‡§∑‡•ã ‡§≤‡§ò‡•Å‡§É ‡§∂‡•Ä‡§§‡§É ‡§ñ‡§∞‡§É ‡§∏‡•Ç‡§ï‡•ç‡§∑‡•ç‡§Æ‡§∂‡•ç‡§ö‡§≤‡•ã‡§Ω‡§®‡§ø‡§≤‡§É‡•§" (AH. Su. 1/11)

1.  **Ruksha (Dry):** 
    - *Physiology:* Absorbs moisture and fats. It is responsible for the dryness in the colon during the formation of stools.
    - *Clinical:* Excessive Ruksha leads to dry skin, constipation, cracked voice, and roughness of organs.
2.  **Laghu (Light):** 
    - *Physiology:* Enables quick movement and agility. It keeps the body channels (Srotas) open.
    - *Clinical:* Excess causes weight loss, insomnia, and a feeling of "floating" or lack of groundedness.
3.  **Sheeta (Cold):** 
    - *Physiology:* Reduces metabolic heat and slows down circulation.
    - *Clinical:* Causes stiffness (Stambha), tremors (Kampa), and intolerance to cold weather. It constricts blood vessels.
4.  **Khara (Rough):** 
    - *Physiology:* Opposite of smooth (Shlakshna). It is responsible for the "breaking" or "tearing" sensation.
    - *Clinical:* Leads to rough skin, cracked heels, and "crepitus" (noise) in joints.
5.  **Sukshma (Subtle):** 
    - *Physiology:* The ability to enter the smallest pores and channels of the body (e.g., capillaries, nerve endings).
    - *Clinical:* This property allows Vata to spread localized pain to the entire body quickly.
6.  **Chala (Mobile):** 
    - *Physiology:* The root of all biological motion (nervous impulses, circulation, excretion).
    - *Clinical:* Excess leads to hyperactivity, anxiety, twitching, and rapid thoughts.

### üî• Pitta Gunas (Properties of Pitta)
> "‡§™‡§ø‡§§‡•ç‡§§‡§Ç ‡§∏‡§∏‡•ç‡§®‡•á‡§π ‡§§‡•Ä‡§ï‡•ç‡§∑‡•ç‡§£‡•ã‡§∑‡•ç‡§£‡§Ç ‡§≤‡§ò‡•Å ‡§µ‡§ø‡§∏‡•ç‡§∞‡§Ç ‡§∏‡§∞‡§Ç ‡§¶‡•ç‡§∞‡§µ‡§Æ‡•ç‡•§" (AH. Su. 1/11)

1.  **Sasneha (Slightly Oily):** 
    - *Physiology:* Unlike Vata's dryness, Pitta has a "moist" heat. This protects the tissues from being completely burnt by the internal fire.
    - *Clinical:* High Sasneha leads to oily skin (especially T-zone) and loose stools.
2.  **Tikshna (Sharp/Penetrating):** 
    - *Physiology:* High metabolic efficiency. It "cuts" through heavy food and complex information.
    - *Clinical:* Causes acidity, ulcers, inflammation, and a sharp, irritable temper.
3.  **Ushna (Hot):** 
    - *Physiology:* Digestion and body temperature regulation.
    - *Clinical:* Leads to fever, excessive sweating, burning sensations (Daha), and inflammation.
4.  **Laghu (Light):** 
    - *Physiology:* Like fire, Pitta moves upward and spreads fast.
    - *Clinical:* Causes dizziness and rapid onset of symptoms.
5.  **Visra (Fleshy Smell):** 
    - *Physiology:* The characteristic metabolic odor.
    - *Clinical:* Strong body odor or foul-smelling urine/stools indicate Pitta aggravation.
6.  **Sara (Flowing/Spreading):** 
    - *Physiology:* Ensures that hormones and enzymes reach their target organs.
    - *Clinical:* Excessive "Sara" property leads to diarrhea (Mridu Koshta).
7.  **Drava (Liquid):** 
    - *Physiology:* Secretion of bile, saliva, and sweat.
    - *Clinical:* Excess leads to water retention in inflammatory areas or excessive thinness of blood.

### üíß Kapha Gunas (Properties of Kapha)
> "‡§∏‡•ç‡§®‡§ø‡§ó‡•ç‡§ß‡§É ‡§∂‡•Ä‡§§‡•ã ‡§ó‡•Å‡§∞‡•Å‡§∞‡•ç‡§Æ‡§®‡•ç‡§¶‡§É ‡§∂‡•ç‡§≤‡§ï‡•ç‡§∑‡•ç‡§£‡•ã ‡§Æ‡•É‡§§‡•ç‡§∏‡•ç‡§®‡§É ‡§∏‡•ç‡§•‡§ø‡§∞‡§É ‡§ï‡§´‡§É‡•§" (AH. Su. 1/12)

1.  **Snigdha (Oily/Unctuous):** 
    - *Physiology:* Lubrication of joints and organs.
    - *Clinical:* Excess leads to oily skin, phlegm production, and obesity.
2.  **Sheeta (Cold):** 
    - *Physiology:* Acts as a coolant against Pitta's heat.
    - *Clinical:* Slows down metabolism and causes congestion/coldness in the chest.
3.  **Guru (Heavy):** 
    - *Physiology:* Provides structural integrity and bulk to bones and muscles.
    - *Clinical:* Leads to lethargy, excessive sleep, and heaviness in the stomach after meals.
4.  **Manda (Slow/Dull):** 
    - *Physiology:* Sustained energy. Slow absorption of nutrients for long-term storage.
    - *Clinical:* Slow thinking, slow digestion (Mandagni), and delayed response.
5.  **Shlakshna (Smooth):** 
    - *Physiology:* Reduces friction in joints and internal linings.
    - *Clinical:* Excess results in excessive mucus and smooth but pale skin.
6.  **Mritsna (Slimy/Sticky):** 
    - *Physiology:* Cohesion. It holds the organs and joints together (like glue).
    - *Clinical:* Causes "sticky" feelings in the throat or mouth and plaque in arteries.
7.  **Sthira (Stable):** 
    - *Physiology:* Endurance and consistency.
    - *Clinical:* Leads to stubbornness, fixed mindsets, and difficulty in changing habits.

## üß¨ 7. Role in Prakruti & Health
### A. Prakruti (Constitution)
- Formed at conception (Shukra-Shonita Samyoga) based on the dominant Dosha.
- It is permanent (like DNA) and determines physical/mental traits.
- **Vata Prakruti:** Lean, fast, creative, anxious.
- **Pitta Prakruti:** Moderate build, sharp intellect, angry, warm.
- **Kapha Prakruti:** Heavy build, calm, slow, steady.

### B. Health Maintenance (Samyavastha)
- **Health:** When Doshas are in equilibrium (*Samadosha*).
- **Disease:** When Doshas are imbalanced (*Vikara*).
- The goal of Ayurveda is to maintain the *Samyavastha* of Tridosha.

## ‚öñÔ∏è 8. Prakrita vs. Vikrita Dosha
1.  **Prakrita Dosha (Physiological):**
    - The natural state of Dosha in the body.
    - Performs normal functions (e.g., Vata causes movement, Pitta digests).
    - **"Avikrita"** (Uncorrupted).
2.  **Vikrita Dosha (Pathological):**
    - The abnormal/aggravated state.
    - Causes disease and symptoms.
    - Moves from its own seat to other tissues (Dhatu Gata).
