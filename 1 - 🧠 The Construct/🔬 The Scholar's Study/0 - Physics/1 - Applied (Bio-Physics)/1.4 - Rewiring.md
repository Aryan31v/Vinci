### 1. Long-Term Potentiation (LTP) = "Thickening the Wire"

4.  **Experience:** What was once "hard" (High $R$) becomes "automatic" (Superconductor).

Why is change so hard?
**Hysteresis** is the dependence of the state of a system on its history.

- **The Path of Least Resistance:** Electricity *always* takes the easiest path.
- **The Trap:** Your old bad habits have thick, insulated, low-resistance wires. Your new desired habit has a thin, high-resistance dirt road.
- **The Struggle:** When you are tired or stressed (Low Voltage), the current will **automatically** default to the old wire.

### âš”ï¸ The Protocol: Overcoming Hysteresis
To build the new wire, you must **Force** current through the High-Resistance path manually.
- This creates **Heat** (Friction/Discomfort).
- **The Grind:** That "I hate this" feeling is literally the sensation of current breaking through high resistance.
- **The Victory:** Once the new receptors are installed (LTP), the resistance drops, and the new habit becomes the "Path of Least Resistance."
