Human reasoning is not based on "Formal Logic" (like a computer); it is based on **Mental Models.** According to Philip Johnson-Laird, we deduce truths by imagining a specific scenario (an example) and checking if the logic holds in that "world." We struggle with abstract rules because they require the construction of multiple competing mental models, which overloads our bandwidth.

- **The Wason Selection Task:** People fail logic puzzles with "A" and "4" but solve them perfectly when they are about "Drinking Age" and "Beer." We are socially and concretely wired.
